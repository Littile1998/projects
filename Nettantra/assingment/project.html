A Performance-to-Cost Analysis of IEEE 802.15.4 MAC With 802.15.4e MAC Modes

The IEEE 802.15.4 standard is one of the widely adopted networking specification for Internet of Things (IoT). It defines several physical layer (PHY) options and medium access control (MAC) sub-layer protocols for interconnection of constrained wireless devices. These devices are usually battery-powered and need to support requirements like low-power consumption and low-data rates.

IEEE 802.15.4e defines five different MAC behaviors, viz., Blink RFID, AMCA, LLDN, DSME, and TSCH. Both RFID and AMCA are the two non-real-time MAC behaviors. Deterministic and Synchronous Multi-channel Extension (DSME) and Time-slotted Channel Hopping (TSCH) MAC modes of IEEE 802.15.4e.

In this paper, we carry out a performance-to-cost analysis of Deterministic and Synchronous Multi-channel Extension (DSME) and Time-slotted Channel Hopping (TSCH) MAC modes of IEEE 802.15.4e with 802.15.4 MAC protocol to analyze the trade-off of choosing a particular MAC mode over others.

Applications of IEEE 802.15.4 and IEEE 802.15.4e:

802.15.4 MAC is suitable for applications with flexible requirements of latency and throughput. Where as, to meet specific Quality-of-Service (QoS) requirements of different industrial applications, the IEEE 802.15.4e amendment was released that focuses on improving reliability, robustness and latency.
